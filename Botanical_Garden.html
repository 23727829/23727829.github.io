Botanical Garden Project

Project Management

For this project, our client Dr. Don Kirkwood (curator of the botanical gardens) had a problem with the functionality of the current botanical garden map, and requested our aid as GIS Expect to make the map more interactive and user friendly. On this project we worked as a group of three, each individual had a unique skill set, so we divided the work amongst us. Rutger attained the role of being the administrator as he has strong leadership skills while Joshua and I dealt more with the technical part since were more experienced.

A six-weeks timeline was agreed upon to complete the project.One of the challenges we experienced in managing the botanical garden project was ensuring that all stakeholders are aligned and working towards the same goals.Also, we used the Gantt chart to organize, sub-divide tasks, and track the progress. This helped us improve our overall efficiency and ensure that we met the deadline.

GIS Strategy & Implementation

The plan was to utilize ArcGIS Pro and the online version for mapping and visual paradigm to compute our conceptual and logical design for our geodatabase.This enabled us to was highlight the important entities involved in the project and implement our strategy.With the conceptual planning,we focused on developing a clear understanding of the project requirements,and created a high-level view of the project.

Data Preparation

A plant collection recorded as an Excel sheet and Building shapefile was provided.However there were missing entities and overlapping polygons thus we had to redigitize a few polygons and also clean the data collection. This was all made possible through excel and ArcGIS Pro Software.

Final product

Once the raw data was processed, analysed and the editing process was completed, the construction phase commenced. The build process of the prototype of an interactive map included the making additional map layers, followed by the actual creation of the map and to adding functionality to it.the interactive features of the map included zooming in and out, panning across the map to explore the map in detail, clicking on individual points to view more information, and toggling between different layers and base maps.

Overall, the project was a great learning experience. Managing a project and meeting goals on time requires precise planning and management. Implementing the right steps at the right time is crucial to the project's overall outcome.
